Conclusion and Future Work

The visualisation design does a satisfactory job of conveying the most important information quickly, concisely and correctly. While elements of it remain unpolished, its overall functionality is complete. The work is generalisable to other periods of war,  whether from Martin’s work, the work of other fantasy authors or real-world.

Future work could look at extending the data set used and the visualisation to include the locations of best-loved/best-hated characters such as Arya Stark and Cersei Lannister when particular battles are taking place. Additionally, implementing the design would be a good way of verifying its usefulness and usability.

Work Split

The during the prototyping phase, every author contributed to the design of each prototype. However, Urisohn was responsible for implementing the map prototype, Borysova the graph prototype and Versfeld the chess prototype.

Both the initial and final presentations were developed by all three authors in unison, but with each author presenting the particular prototype they had implemented.

All three contributed to the final design, which was then implemented by Urisohn. Borysova and Versfeld headed up the implementation of the website. The authors contributed equally to the writing of this report.
